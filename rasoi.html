SELF HELP GROUP

Self-Help Group refers to self-governed, peer controlled, informal group of people with same socio-economic background and having a desire to collectively perform common purposes. Here poor people voluntarily come together to save whatever amount they can save conveniently out of their earnings, to mutually agree to contribute to a common fund and to lend to the members for meeting their productive and emergent needs.

SHG is a holistic programme of micro-enterprises covering all aspects of self-employment, organization of the rural poor into self help groups and their capacity building, planning of activity clusters, infrastructure build up, technology, credit and marketing.

WHAT IS SHG?

  • A self-help group (SHG) is a financial intermediary owned by the poor.
  • Usually comprising of more than 8 women, it acts as a way of facilitating informal financials services to rural poor.
  • It is an intricate social design in which people participate by making themselves socially and financially accountable to each other.
  • Similar to ROSCA’s in several other countries, wherein homogeneous group members start similar financial initiatives.
  • To help such women become a part of rapid nation growth.

PURPOSE OF PPSHG

  • To promote women empowerment.
  • To spread awareness amongst women regarding their rights.
  • To unleash theirpotential which is hidden due to scarcity.
  • To promoteliteracy and education amongst women.
  • To promotesocialism in such under-developed areas.
  • To fill in leadership qualities in women.
  • To help such women become a part of rapid nation growth.
  • To provide marginalized women (dalits, prostitutes, AID’ s, disabled, tribal, widows) necessities for their development.
  • To help women create their own capital by building their saving habits.

WORK OF PPSHG

  • The members of the PPSHG will take such people into count who are deprived of the basic facilities but on the other hand, they are willing to build up themselves.
  • PPSHG will form a self help group of more than 8 such women to provide them training which will help them to employ themselves.
  • PPSHG will manage the availability of micro-credits on their own or from banks which will reduce the burden from the women.
  • PPSHG will also help the already formed groups and provide them training and micro-credits without taking any charges.
  • PPSHG, after providing funds and training, will also provide them training certificates and employment or help them to start-up a small business on their own.
